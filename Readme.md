The Enteric Nervous System as a Somatic Computation Channel

*Working Paper --- Formalized from structured dialogue*

> *The gut does not think. The gut provides light.*

**Core Thesis**

The human body contains a private, analog computation channel that
operates below conscious access most of the time. The channel's carrier
signal is the enteric nervous system: an autonomous, spatially
structured, involuntary neural mesh that broadcasts continuously to the
brain via the vagus nerve. The brain encodes and retrieves cognitive
states against this carrier via predictive error computation in the
insular cortex. Contemplative traditions have independently discovered
how to tune into it. Sustained interoceptive attention determines the
channel's depth.

This is a claim about the somatic basis of intuition. It is stated
plainly because it deserves to be evaluated plainly. The engineering
language that follows is in service of this claim, not a substitute for
it.

Every anatomical component required for this architecture is documented
in existing neuroscience. No novel anatomy is proposed. No novel
connections, cell types, or signaling mechanisms. The only novel claim
is what the system is doing. This document is a conceptual framework,
not an empirical report. It argues that known gut--brain circuitry
supports a function beyond homeostatic regulation, identifies specific
experiments that would discriminate this function from simpler
alternatives, and proposes that sustained interoceptive attention
develops the channel's receiver. Throughout, claims of architectural
compatibility are distinguished from claims of positive evidence.

**1. The Carrier Signal**

The myenteric plexus is a 500-million-neuron mesh that generates
complex, stereotyped spatiotemporal patterns---peristalsis,
segmentation, tonic accommodation, the migrating motor complex---from
local rules, without central orchestration. These patterns are broadcast
continuously to the brainstem via vagal afferents, approximately 90% of
which are sensory.

Five properties distinguish the enteric signal from all other candidate
carriers:

**Autonomic independence.** The ENS operates with the vagus severed. The
carrier is generated independently of central state, ensuring decoupling
between the reference signal and cortical content.

**Spatial complexity.** The plexus contains hundreds of thousands of
ganglia distributed across a two-dimensional surface, connected by gap
junctions, ephaptic fields, and volume transmission---three mechanisms
that delocalize information across the mesh before vagal sampling. The
carrier is a field, not a wire.

**Temporal structure.** The ENS generates a small vocabulary of
stereotyped patterns, each locally stable on its own timescale:
peristaltic waves over seconds, segmentation at \~0.2 Hz, tonic
accommodation over minutes, the MMC over \~90 minutes. Each mode
functions as a local invariant---a stable reference against which
deviations become information.

**Involuntary operation.** Enteric motility cannot be voluntarily
overridden. The cortex cannot shape the carrier to produce desired
readouts. This is not incidental but formally required: a reference
signal modifiable by the reader is an incorruptible-channel violation.

**Metabolic gratuity.** The carrier runs because digestion requires it.
The signal is a byproduct of a process that operates regardless of
cognitive use. Bandwidth is free.

No other autonomic subsystem satisfies all five constraints. Cardiac
signals are spatially one-dimensional. Respiratory signals admit
voluntary override. Electrodermal signals lack temporal structure. Only
the ENS combines the full configuration.

Status: The five properties are individually established. Their
conjunction uniquely identifies the ENS as a candidate carrier. This is
a compatibility claim: the ENS could serve this function. The evidence
that it does is developed in subsequent sections.

**2. The Delocalized Field**

The enteric carrier is not a discrete signal transmitted point-to-point.
Three physical mechanisms ensure that local events delocalize across the
mesh before vagal readout:

**Gap junction coupling** provides direct electrical connections between
enteric neurons, passing graded (analog) potentials laterally through
the tissue.

**Ephaptic coupling** allows neurons packed tightly enough that their
electrical fields influence neighbors without synaptic or gap junction
contact---propagation determined by geometry rather than wiring.

**Volume transmission** releases neurotransmitters into extracellular
space rather than into synapses, creating a broadcast medium that
affects all cells within diffusion range.

The vagal afferent terminals that sample this field---intraganglionic
laminar endings (IGLEs)---are not point sensors. They are leaf-shaped
structures that drape over myenteric ganglia, integrating across the
surface of each node. Each IGLE samples a local patch of an
already-delocalized field.

If the field is delocalized before sampling, then each vagal afferent
carries information about the global state, not merely its local
neighborhood. The compression ratio between enteric neurons and vagal
fibers does not bound the information in the way a point-sampling
bottleneck would. Each fragment contains a degraded image of the whole.
Fidelity degrades gracefully rather than collapsing categorically.

This is the property that gives holography its name: whole-writing. The
enteric field and optical holograms are instances of the same structural
class---whole-pattern encoding in a distributed medium, recoverable from
fragments. The medium differs. The property does not. The term is
etymological, not analogical: holo- (whole) + -graphy (writing).

Status: Gap junctions, ephaptic coupling, and volume transmission in the
myenteric plexus are established. IGLE morphology is well characterized.
However, the three delocalization mechanisms operate at very different
spatial scales and timescales. Gap junction coupling in enteric ganglia
is relatively local. Ephaptic effects fall off sharply with distance.
Volume transmission has a wider radius but is slow and noisy. Whether
these mechanisms collectively produce coherence across the full gut
surface---rather than just local neighborhoods---is not just an open
question but the question the entire holographic claim depends on. If
delocalization is only local, the framework does not produce
whole-pattern encoding; it produces a collection of loosely correlated
local signals, which is a much weaker carrier. The spatial scale of
coherence the framework requires has not been quantified, and this is
its most significant empirical vulnerability. The "holographic" label
names the structural property being claimed, not a demonstrated physical
regime.

**3. The Carrier as Invariant**

In echolocation, the speed of sound is not the signal. It is the
invariant---the constant that makes the system work because it does not
change, so everything that does change becomes information. The bat does
not process the speed of sound. The bat processes deviations that are
detectable only because the speed of sound is reliable.

The enteric carrier serves the same structural role. Its autonomic,
stereotyped, predictable character makes it the invariant against which
cortical states register as deviations.

But the enteric carrier exceeds the echolocation case in a fundamental
respect. The speed of sound is a single constant defining a single
detection space. The enteric system generates a repertoire of
simultaneously active invariants: peristaltic waves, segmentation
oscillations, tonic accommodation, MMC cycling---each locally stable on
its own timescale, each defining its own subspace of detectable
deviation.

A constant gives one axis of detection. A signal that is locally
constant in multiple distinct modes gives as many axes as there are
modes. The carrier is not merely a reference signal. It is a coordinate
system. The dimensionality of the readout space is multiplied by the
number of simultaneously active invariants.

Status: "Invariant" and "coordinate system" are used here as structural
descriptions, not mathematical formalizations. A full formalization
would require specifying the state space of enteric patterns, the metric
on that space, and the mapping between carrier states and detection
subspaces. What is claimed is the structural observation that multiple
concurrent stable modes provide a richer detection space than a single
reference---which follows from the carrier properties established in
Section 1.

**4. The Existing Comparator Architecture**

The cortical architecture required for carrier-based readout is
documented, published, and accepted under a different functional
interpretation.

The posterior and mid insular cortex receive primary interoceptive
representations relayed from the nucleus tractus solitarius. The
anterior insular cortex operates as a comparator or prediction error
module. Agranular visceromotor cortices---including the anterior
cingulate and ventral anterior insula---issue interoceptive predictions
downward to the brainstem while simultaneously sending those predictions
to the mid-posterior insula, where they meet ascending afferent signals.
The prediction arrives. The actual signal arrives. The comparison
happens in the same tissue. The prediction error is the output.

This architecture has been characterized under the Embodied Predictive
Interoception Coding (EPIC) model (Barrett & Simmons, 2015) and the
interoceptive predictive coding model of conscious presence (Seth,
Suzuki & Critchley, 2011). Both describe the insula as computing
prediction errors against visceral afferents within a hierarchical
Bayesian framework. Bidirectional connections link the insular cortex to
regions involved in cognition, memory, salience, and
self-representation---including amygdala, hippocampus, nucleus
accumbens, and orbitofrontal cortex.

The existing literature assigns this architecture a homeostatic
function: the brain predicts body states to regulate them. The
experimental paradigms that support this assignment demonstrate that the
insula tracks visceral signals and computes mismatches. They do not
demonstrate what the mismatches are for. The architecture was
discovered. A function was assigned. The assignment is reasonable but
has not been experimentally discriminated from the alternative proposed
here.

**4.1 Why Not Simpler Explanations?**

Several established mechanisms can account for portions of the
phenomenology this framework addresses. Interoceptive modulation of
attention and arousal, somatic markers influencing decision confidence,
and generic state-dependent recall each explain some of what is
attributed here to visceral holography. The question is whether these
accounts are sufficient or whether the enteric signal's specific
computational properties do additional work.

The framework's distinctive claim is not that the gut influences
cognition---that is well established---but that the enteric signal's
spatial structure supports a parallel, high-dimensional readout that
generic arousal or valence modulation cannot produce. Somatic marker
theory assigns the gut a scalar influence: approach or avoid, weighted
by intensity. State-dependent recall indexes memory to physiological
context but does not propose that the indexing medium has internal
spatial structure that determines readout dimensionality. The present
framework claims that the carrier's spatial complexity does specific
computational work---not merely biasing cognition, but structuring what
can be retrieved and how.

The discriminating prediction: if visceral holography is operative, then
manipulations that preserve arousal and valence but disrupt the spatial
structure of the enteric signal should selectively impair the
high-dimensional readout without affecting generic somatic marker
function. Hypothesis H4 is designed to test this distinction.

**5. Neural Reuse**

Neural reuse is among the most established principles in neuroscience.
The visual cortex was not evolved for reading. Broca's area was not
evolved for music. The hippocampus was not evolved for imagination.
Neural hardware is routinely repurposed by any system that can exploit
its computational properties.

The visceral holography claim requires less repurposing than any of
these cases. The enteric carrier is already broadcasting. The insular
cortex is already computing prediction errors against it. The vagal
pathway is already transmitting spatial field information. No signal
needs to be redirected. No region needs to be recruited. The only
addition is what gets encoded at the convergence zone---and encoding is
a function of attention, not anatomy.

It does not matter whether the interoceptive prediction error system
evolved for homeostatic regulation, for associative cognitive readout,
or for something else. The architecture has specific computational
properties, and those properties are available to any process that
interfaces with them. Nothing prevents the system from being used this
way even if it was not originally for this. That is what neural reuse
means.

**6. Storage: The Negative Complement**

The proposed storage mechanism is standard associative learning at
convergence neurons that receive both vagal afferent and descending
cortical input simultaneously. During moments of concurrent
salience---when the enteric carrier state and a cortical state are
co-attended with sufficient intensity to drive synaptic
modification---an interference pattern is deposited. This pattern is
termed a negative complement: a coupling matrix between the carrier's
spatial pattern and the cortical activation state, shaped like the
inverse of the expected vagal input at the time of encoding.

When the current carrier state matches the state active during encoding,
the stored negative produces a prediction error: the delta between
expected and actual vagal input. Because the comparison occurs across
the full topographic map simultaneously, the readout is parallel. This
architecture is structurally analogous to a matched filter---the optimal
linear detector for a known signal in noise---though whether the neural
implementation achieves matched-filter optimality or merely approximates
it is unknown and depends on the storage mechanism (see Section 6.1).

Different carrier modes---peristaltic, segmental, tonic, MMC---function
as different reference states. Each mode illuminates a different library
of stored negatives. This predicts state-dependent accessibility:
certain cognitive reconstructions available only in certain
physiological states, because the reference must match the encoding
conditions.

**6.1 Open Engineering Problems**

The storage mechanism is described here in functional terms. Translating
it into a testable computational model requires addressing several
problems that this framework identifies but does not solve:

**Capacity.** How many negatives can the convergence zone hold? The
insular cortex is a large structure with extensive cortical columns, but
associative memory capacity scales with the number of independent
storage sites and the dimensionality of the stored patterns. Standard
Hopfield network analysis suggests capacity scales linearly with the
number of neurons but degrades as stored patterns begin to interfere.
Whether the insular convergence zone behaves like a Hopfield network, a
sparse distributed memory, or something else is unknown.

**Overwriting and interference.** Carrier states recur---the MMC cycles
every 90 minutes, peristaltic patterns repeat continuously. When a
familiar carrier state recurs in a new cortical context, what happens to
the previously stored negative? Three possibilities: overwriting (the
old negative is replaced, producing a recency bias in the readout),
blurring (the old and new negatives average, producing a generalized
readout that reflects the statistical ensemble of cortical states paired
with that carrier mode), or multiplexing (the convergence zone stores
multiple negatives against the same carrier state, disambiguated by
partial cortical context at retrieval). Each produces different
phenomenology. Overwriting predicts that intuitions are dominated by
recent experience. Blurring predicts that intuitions reflect long-run
patterns rather than specific episodes. Multiplexing predicts that the
same somatic signal can reconstruct different cognitive states depending
on which cortical context is partially active at retrieval.
Introspective reports are most consistent with a mixture of blurring and
multiplexing, but introspective reports are not evidence.

**Reconstruction fidelity.** How closely must the current carrier state
match the encoding state for reconstruction to occur? A strict matching
requirement would mean that negatives are accessible only during
near-exact recurrence of the encoding carrier---a narrow retrieval
window. A graded matching function would allow partial reconstruction
from approximate carrier matches, with fidelity degrading smoothly as
the match weakens. The holographic analogy predicts graded degradation
(partial illumination reconstructs a blurry but complete image), but
whether the neural implementation exhibits this property is unknown.

**Noise model.** The matched-filter description implies that the readout
is optimal for detecting a known signal in noise. What is the noise?
Candidates include: cardiac and respiratory artifact in the vagal
signal, stochastic variation in enteric motility, concurrent cortical
activity unrelated to the stored pattern, and thermal noise in synaptic
transmission. The relative contribution of each noise source determines
where the system's performance bottleneck lies and what kinds of
interference degrade readout most. This is the most technically
tractable of the open problems---it could be estimated from simultaneous
EGG, ECG, and respiratory recording during interoceptive tasks.

These are not rhetorical questions. They are the engineering problems
that must be solved to advance from framework to model. The present
document identifies them. The next version should begin to answer them.

**7. Attention Writes the Film**

Hardware determines the ceiling. Attention writes the film.

The stored negatives are deposited during moments when the enteric
carrier and a cortical state are co-attended at the interoceptive
convergence zone with sufficient salience to drive synaptic
modification. Each such moment is one exposure.

Most individuals never attend to the enteric signal at the resolution
required for high-fidelity exposure. The signal arrives, is processed
subcortically, and produces coarse interoceptive labels. The convergence
zone never receives the carrier at sufficient resolution to write
fine-grained negatives. The holographic medium exists but remains
largely unexposed.

Sustained interoceptive attention---practiced over years across diverse
cognitive and physiological states---writes a large, high-resolution
library of negatives. The resolution of stored negatives determines the
dimensionality of the readout. The basis set grows with the diversity of
carrier-cortical pairings that have been attended to.

This predicts that the channel is developed, not innate. An individual
with high autonomic sensitivity who never attends has the hardware but
no exposures. An individual with average sensitivity who attends
carefully develops a lower-resolution but functional channel. An
individual with both exceptional sensitivity and sustained practice
develops maximum fidelity.

Contemplative traditions are a consistent data point. The instruction
across meditation lineages---direct attention to visceral sensation,
stop controlling the breath, attend to what the body
carries---translates directly into the present framework: stop shaping
the carrier, let it run clean, attend to what it carries. The alignment
is noted, not claimed as evidence; other accounts of meditative
development produce similar predictions. A discriminating test would be
whether contemplative practitioners show increased gastric-cortical
phase coupling (H3) rather than merely improved generic attentional
metrics.

**8. Phenomenological Alignment**

The following observations describe the phenomenology the framework was
designed to account for. They are offered as alignment: the theory
produces predictions consistent with these experiences. Post hoc
consistency between a theory and its motivating observations is
necessary but not sufficient. A theory that failed to account for its
motivating data would be immediately disqualified; a theory that
succeeds may still be wrong. Of the full set of phenomenological
alignments the framework produces, only the three with the most specific
or discriminating predictions are presented here. Each names the
alternative account that produces the same prediction and identifies
where the framework's prediction diverges.

**Intuition is state-dependent.** Different carrier modes illuminate
different negative libraries. Alignment: consistent with reported
experience and with generic state-dependent memory. The discriminating
test is H4: whether gastric motility phase specifically indexes recall,
not merely arousal or metabolic state.

**Clarity correlates with gastric quiescence.** Active peristalsis
degrades carrier coherence and reconstruction fidelity. Alignment:
consistent. Metabolic resource competition already explains postprandial
cognitive degradation. The framework predicts this correlation survives
when metabolic load is controlled---a testable distinction.

**Compression without loss.** The readout is a high-dimensional gestalt
that resists verbal decomposition. The phenomenology of knowing more
than you can say is the gap between readout dimensionality and verbal
projection dimensionality. Alignment: matches. Dual-process theory's
implicit/explicit distinction produces a similar phenomenological
prediction.

**9. What Is Not Claimed**

The ENS does not think. No gut-based cognition is proposed. The
operative computation remains cortical. The ENS provides a carrier
signal with properties the brain can exploit for associative
readout---not a second brain.

Optical holography is not proposed as mechanism. "Visceral holography"
and "optical holography" are instances of the same structural
class---whole-pattern encoding in a distributed medium---sharing a
genus, not a mechanism. The term is etymological: holo- (whole) +
-graphy (writing).

Mathematical formalization is not yet achieved. Terms like "matched
filter," "coordinate system," and "bandwidth" are structural
descriptions of the proposed architecture's operational logic, not
defined quantities in a formal model. Section 6.1 identifies the
specific engineering problems that must be solved to advance to
formalization. The present document identifies what needs to be
formalized; it does not complete the formalization.

A general population-level claim is not made. The architecture is
proposed as generally available. Individual variation in the channel's
fidelity is predicted but not yet characterized. Because the carrier is
generated by the myenteric plexus, and the plexus scales with gastric
mucosal surface area, constitutional variation in gastric volume
predicts variation in carrier bandwidth. Individuals with larger enteric
substrates would, on this framework, have access to a higher-dimensional
carrier---though whether this translates to measurably different
cognitive readout is an empirical question the framework identifies but
does not answer.

**10. What Is Claimed**

The body contains a private, analog computation channel. Its carrier is
the enteric nervous system---autonomous, spatially complex, involuntary,
temporally structured, and metabolically free. The brain's insular
cortex already computes prediction errors against this carrier using
documented architecture. The existing functional assignment of this
architecture (homeostatic regulation) is consistent with the evidence
but has not been experimentally distinguished from the alternative
proposed here: that the system also supports associative cognitive
retrieval, producing the phenomenology of somatic intuition.

The channel's depth varies with attentional practice. The experience
contemplative traditions describe---attention to visceral sensation
producing something that feels more like knowing than feeling---is what
the framework predicts, though this alignment is not by itself
discriminating.

This claim is currently underdetermined by the evidence marshaled. The
framework demonstrates architectural compatibility and generates
discriminating predictions. It does not yet demonstrate that the
proposed function is operative. That is a more interesting problem to
have than incoherence, and it has a clear solution: run the experiments.

**11. Discriminating Experiments**

The following hypotheses are designed to discriminate the framework from
simpler alternatives. Each identifies a prediction that visceral
holography makes and that generic interoceptive modulation, somatic
marker theory, or state-dependent recall do not, or make differently.

**H1. Carrier coherence predicts pattern-recognition performance.**
Gastric EGG phase coherence should correlate positively with rapid
pattern-recognition performance in individuals with demonstrated
interoceptive accuracy, but not in controls. Simpler alternatives
predict arousal or valence modulation of performance but not modulation
indexed to gastric phase coherence specifically.

**H2. Accommodation amplifies insular prediction error.** Gastric
accommodation should correlate with higher interoceptive
prediction-error amplitudes at the insula (fMRI) compared to active
peristaltic states. Simpler alternatives predict insular activation
correlated with interoceptive salience generally, not with the coherence
of the gastric signal specifically.

**H3. Interoceptive training increases carrier-cortical coupling.**
Sustained interoceptive attention should increase mutual information
between gastric EGG phase and cortical EEG phase at interoceptive
convergence regions over weeks. Generic meditation effects (reduced
arousal, improved focus) do not predict increased gastric-cortical phase
coupling specifically.

**H4. Motility-phase-indexed state-dependent recall.** Information
encoded during gastric accommodation should be more accessible during
accommodation than during active peristalsis, and vice versa,
independent of metabolic state, arousal level, and mood. This is the
critical test. Generic state-dependent recall predicts indexing by
arousal, metabolic state, or context. Only the carrier framework
predicts indexing by gastric motility phase specifically. A positive
result would constitute strong evidence that the enteric signal is doing
spatial-structural encoding work. A negative result would substantially
weaken the framework.
